## Layout Contract

- Home screen must read as a map poster first and an application shell second.
- The world map occupies the visual majority of the viewport on both desktop and mobile.
- Chrome is intentionally sparse: one main title, one guiding line, the map, and the point preview drawer.
- No stats, KPI cards, toolbars, onboarding modal, or secondary content blocks in Phase 1.
- Desktop layout: title block sits above or lightly over the map canvas; drawer anchors to the right edge and remains closed until a point is clicked.
- Mobile layout: title block remains compact at the top; drawer becomes a bottom sheet and remains closed until a point is clicked.
- Map container must preserve a calm framed composition rather than edge-to-edge clutter. Keep visible negative space around the title block and drawer edge.

## Surface And Texture Contract

- Base map must feel like aged paper rather than flat vector UI. Use warm parchment surfaces, subtle grain, and low-contrast coastline or border strokes.
- Texture intensity stays restrained: visible at rest, never noisy enough to compete with markers or copy.
- Ink color for text and map lines should be dark brown rather than pure black to keep the poster tone cohesive.
- Cards and drawer surfaces should look slightly denser than the map surface so the preview content reads clearly without breaking the vintage palette.
- Shadows should be soft and dusty, not glossy. Avoid glassmorphism, neon fills, or saturated gradients.

---

## Marker Contract

- Phase 1 shows only a few seed points so the map feels alive immediately on first load.
- Seed points use a subtle glow and restrained fill, not a loud dashboard-style beacon.
- Future user-created points must be visually distinguishable later, so seed points should establish the quieter style now.
- Only a small curated subset of seed points may show persistent labels. All other seed points stay unlabeled until selected.
- Default seed point:
  - small filled circle in muted earth tone
  - soft outer glow at low opacity
  - no permanent heavy ring
- Hover or focus state:
  - slight scale increase and glow lift only
  - do not change the entire map palette on hover
- Selected state:
  - add a clear accent ring and stronger glow
  - selected point becomes the highest-contrast marker on screen
  - if the point has a label, the label border can adopt the accent keyline

---

## Drawer Contract

- Drawer is closed by default on initial load.
- Drawer opens only when the user clicks a visible point in Phase 1.
- Desktop drawer anchors on the right and reads as a preview card attached to the map.
- Mobile drawer rises from the bottom and reads as a compact preview sheet.
- Drawer content priority:
  1. place name
  2. country or region line
  3. coordinate line
  4. short preview description
- Drawer tone is preview-first, not form-first. Avoid dense input treatments, multi-action footers, or edit-heavy scaffolding in this phase.
- Close affordance should be present but visually quiet. The map remains the hero.
- When no point is selected, the drawer stays hidden instead of occupying reserved empty space.

---

## Interaction Contract

- Primary interaction in Phase 1 is point selection on the map.
- Clicking a seed point must do three things in one sequence:
  1. promote that point to selected state
  2. open the responsive drawer
  3. populate the drawer with that point's preview data
- Clicking a different seed point replaces the current selection and reuses the same drawer container.
- Initial page load must show the map and seed points immediately after built-in data is read; no autoplay drawer opening.
- Map background clicks do not create points in Phase 1.
- Because projection and recognition are deferred, the map interaction surface should avoid implying free placement or editing.
- Labels should not jitter or reflow dramatically when selection changes; keep motion short and minimal.
